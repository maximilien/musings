My career focused on pioneering software platforms across several technology waves: from early web services, to cloud computing, to containers and serverless, and now to multiagent systems.

**Cloud computing pioneer.** I was a key leader in the development of Cloud Foundry, helping establish it as a leading Platform-as-a-Service. **Research foundation.** My journey began as a principal engineer at IBM Research Triangle Park, where I conducted pioneering work on semantic Web services. ## Why the Bay Area Matters

I've lived and worked in the Bay Area for twenty years. In that time, I've witnessed the rise and fall of multiple technology waves: Web 2.0, mobile, cloud, crypto, and now AI. Each wave brought its own promises and disappointments, its own winners and casualties.

Through it all, I've stayed deeply connected to the startup, meetup, and open source communities that make this region unique. The informal networks here -- the coffee shop conversations, the dinner party debates, the chance encounters at meetups -- accelerate learning in ways that are hard to replicate elsewhere.

Does location still matter in the age of remote work? For many things, no. But for being at the frontier of a rapidly evolving field like AI Agents? I believe it does. The density of talent, capital, and conversation creates a flywheel that's hard to match.

More on this in a future post -- I have a lot more to say about what makes the Bay Area special (and what doesn't).
